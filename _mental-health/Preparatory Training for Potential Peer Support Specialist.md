<h3><strong>Overview</strong></h3>
<p>This is the first module of the "Peer Support Specialist Programme". It
provides learners with an awareness and introduction to the role of a Peer
Support Specialist, and the significance of sharing their lived experiences
in order to support others on their recovery journeys. The module will
be conducted over two days in a classroom.</p>
<p>However, completion of the ‘Preparatory Training for Potential Peer Support
Specialist” module does not guarantee learners a place in the ‘Certificate
in Peer Support’ module.</p>
<h3><strong>Target Audience</strong></h3>
<p>Learners who wish to enrol in this module should:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Be at least 21 years old.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Be keen to know more about peer support work and the role of a Peer Support
Specialist.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Have a diagnosed mental health condition which has stabilised.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Be willing to share their own lived experiences in order to support others
in their recovery.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Be a Singapore Citizen or Singapore Permanent Resident.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>The skills and knowledge that learners are required to possess prior to
applying for this course, are as follows:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>3 GCE ‘O’ level credits, including English, or equivalent qualifications
are preferred.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>An ability to comprehend, speak, read and write English at a proficiency
level equivalent to level 5 of the Employability Skills Workforce Skills
Qualifications (ES WSQ) Workplace Literacy (WPL).</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Numeracy skills at a proficiency level equivalent to level 5 of the Employability
Skills Workforce Skills Qualifications (ES WSQ) Workplace Numeracy (WPN).</p>
</li>
</ul>
<h3><strong>Learning Outcomes</strong></h3>
<p>Upon completion of this module, learners are expected to be able to:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Describe the service provided by a Peer Support Specialist in a mental
health setting.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Describe the key principles of recovery and resilience.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Describe the interaction guidelines in the Peer Support Discipline.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Describe the approach of sharing and writing a recovery story from the
resilience perspective.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<h3><strong>Assessment Requirements</strong></h3>
<p>At the end of the module, participants will be assessed based on a written
assessment.</p>
